The subject is who or what does the verb.

The complete subject of a sentence has all the words that tell who or what the sentence is about.
Examples:

  • Everyone in my house is keeping a secret.
  • The old wooden chair broke underneath him.

The simple subject is the main word or words in the complete subject.
Examples:

  • The old wooden chair broke underneath him.
  • The old radio, that had been quiet for a while, suddenly gave out a disturbing message
  • Around the houses were several hostile indians.

*Sometimes the complete subject and the simple subject are the same.
Examples:

  • Jake practiced and daydreamed for months before the game.
  • Where am I?
  • Who phoned John?
  • Who did John phone?
  • There was nothing so Very remarkable in that; 

A compound subject contains two or more subjects that have the same verb(s). The simple subjects in a compound subject are usually joined by and or or.
Examples:

  • John and Mary are going to the cinema
  • She and I will go to the dance next week.
